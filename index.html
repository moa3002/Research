About Our Project

This project aims to enhance self-regulation skills among first-year secondary students at STEM School in Minia by integrating technological applications into the learning process. The study focuses on four core skills: goal setting, time management, problem-solving, and self-monitoring, which are essential for academic success in a technology-driven environment. The intervention uses Notion, a digital platform that supports students in organizing their learning tasks, managing their time, and tracking their progress. Through structured sessions and practical activities, the project seeks to provide students with the tools and strategies to become more independent, motivated learners. The study also highlights the importance of adopting technology-based solutions in education to improve academic performance and prepare students for future challenges. . .
